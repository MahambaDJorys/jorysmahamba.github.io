Research papers

  • Mathematical Exploration of Phase Transitions in the Ising Model for its relevance in University-level Statistical Physics Teaching (Undergraduate Research Internship Dissertation): june 2024
    • Supervised by Dr. Paul Dario, I worked on the mathematical foundations of the Ising model through the phase transition phenomena it tries to explain.
    • The primary aim of this paper is to offer a course sample for ambitious undergraduate mathematics students interested in applying abstract mathematical theories — such as measure theory — to tangible physical problems, exemplified by the Curie phenomena, which is achieved through sophisticated modelizations, specifically the Ising model. The goal was to produce such a course maintaining the rigorous framework based on definitions, theorems, and proofs.

Interests & Hobbies

Along with sciences and sports, I have a wide range of interests that keep me busy and inspired:

  • Learning: I always commited to continuous learning and tried to find some time each day to learn about something interesting. Now at ESSEC BS, I have the opportunity to explore a wide range of MOOCs for free, allowing me to delve into both practical and fascinating subjects. I'm always on the lookout for new knowledge that enriches my personal and academic life.
  • Philosophy: My love for philosophy began in twelfth grade, sparked by an inspiring lecture from Dr. Stéphane Floccari, who particularily emphasized the importance of daily reflection. This interest deepened during my first year of CPGE when I encountered Nietzsche, whose ideas resonated profoundly with me and made me realize that I had been engaging in philosophical reflection since childhood. Since then, I've explored various philosophies that align with my worldview, such as Stoic pantheism, and grappled with differing views like Rousseau's interpretation of the ancient Chinese Theory of Good Human Nature. Anime also play a significant role in my philosophical journey, with characters like Monkey D. Luffy from One Piece who carries a strong ideal of Liberty and inspires me daily.
  • Classical architecture: My fascination with classical architecture began with the awe-inspiring columns of Columbia University's library. This early experience sparked a lifelong interest in classical and neoclassical architecture. I admire how these styles have influenced iconic institutions like MIT, Harvard, and Columbia, bringing timeless elegance to modern education.
  • The Art of Tea: My passion for tea has led me to explore its rich traditions, from cultivation and transformation to preparation and tasting. One of my dreams is to open my own tea shop in my old days, where I can share my love for this exquisite beverage with others.
  • Urban culture: Growing up in the Paris suburbs as a mixed-race individual, I was deeply influenced by urban culture. Rap music, impactful films and TV Shows, and sports played a significant role in shaping my worldview. Icons such as 2Pac, Eddie Murphy and LeBron James, along with visual medias like The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air or The Pursuit of Hapyness, have all contributed to my understanding of diverse perspectives and experiences.
